T-test

One sample t-test

#simulate data
set.seed(150)
df <- data.frame(RT = rnorm(100, mean = 53, sd = 10))

t.test(df$RT, mu = 53)
## 
##  One Sample t-test
## 
## data:  df$RT
## t = 0.15475, df = 99, p-value = 0.8773
## alternative hypothesis: true mean is not equal to 53
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  51.10630 55.21405
## sample estimates:
## mean of x 
##  53.16018

Students T-test

df_istt <- readxl::read_excel('../Data/is-t-test.xlsx')
tt.results <- t.test(Depression~Group, data=df_istt,
                     var.equal = T)

print(tt.results)
## 
##  Two Sample t-test
## 
## data:  Depression by Group
## t = -5.2112, df = 50, p-value = 3.57e-06
## alternative hypothesis: true difference in means between group One and group Two is not equal to 0
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  -21.634046  -9.596723
## sample estimates:
## mean in group One mean in group Two 
##          46.57692          62.19231

Welchs T-test

Can be used if the assumption of equal variances is violated

df_istt <- readxl::read_excel('../Data/is-t-test.xlsx')
tt.results <- t.test(Depression~Group, data=df_istt, 
                              var.equal = F)

print(tt.results)
## 
##  Welch Two Sample t-test
## 
## data:  Depression by Group
## t = -5.2112, df = 44.913, p-value = 4.56e-06
## alternative hypothesis: true difference in means between group One and group Two is not equal to 0
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  -21.650984  -9.579786
## sample estimates:
## mean in group One mean in group Two 
##          46.57692          62.19231

Correlation Analysis

  • cor() for a correlation matrix
  • cor.test() for a correlation test (Pearsons, Spearman, Kendall)
    • includes p-values etc.

Useful alternatives

  • corr package
  • correlation package

Pearsons Product-Moment Correlation

df_cr <- readRDS('../Data/pcor.RDs')

# Correlation analysis:

cor.test(~ RSpan + Raven, data = df_cr)
## 
##  Pearson's product-moment correlation
## 
## data:  RSpan and Raven
